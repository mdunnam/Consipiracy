OPERATION NORTHWOODS

In 1962, the Joint Chiefs of Staff unanimously approved a plan to conduct terror attacks on US soil — shooting civilians, sinking boats, bombing buildings — and blame Cuba to justify an invasion. President Kennedy rejected it. The document is declassified. It is real.

The Document

Operation Northwoods was a classified Department of Defense proposal produced by the Joint Chiefs of Staff (JCS) and presented to Secretary of Defense Robert McNamara on March 13, 1962. The document — "Justification for US Military Intervention in Cuba" — was declassified and released by the National Security Archive and the Assassination Records Review Board in 1997-2001. It is available in full through the National Security Archive. It is not disputed. It is an official US government document proposing government-sponsored false flag terrorism against American citizens.

The document proposed: (1) "Sink a boatload of Cubans en route to Florida" — using a real boat with real Cuban refugees, sinking it and blaming Cuba; (2) "Blowing up a US ship in Guantanamo Bay and blame Cuba"; (3) "Shooting down a US drone aircraft and blame Cuba"; (4) "Explosion of plastic bomb in a shopping mall in Miami" — staging a bomb attack; (5) "Sabotage of US aircraft" at Florida airports; (6) Developing a "Communist Cuba Terror Campaign" in Miami and Washington including "mock funerals" for staged attacks. All measures were specifically intended to create public support for a US military invasion of Cuba.

The JCS Approved It Unanimously

The Joint Chiefs of Staff — the Chairman and all service chiefs, the highest military authority in the United States — approved the Northwoods document unanimously. General Lyman Lemnitzer, JCS Chairman, signed it. It was presented to Secretary of Defense McNamara as an official military recommendation. President Kennedy rejected it outright. Within months, Kennedy had clashed with the CIA over the Bay of Pigs failure, clashed with the Pentagon over the Cuban Missile Crisis approach, and issued NSAM-263 planning a Vietnam withdrawal. He was assassinated in November 1963.
