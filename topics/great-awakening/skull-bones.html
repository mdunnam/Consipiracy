SKULL & BONES

Yale University's Order 322 — founded 1832. 15 new members ("Bonesmen") tapped each year from Yale's most socially connected senior class. Total membership: approximately 800 living members at any time. CIA directors, senators, presidents, and cabinet members — at a frequency that statistically cannot be random.

The Order

Skull and Bones — officially "The Order of Death" and known internally as "The Order" or "322" — was founded at Yale in 1832 by William Huntington Russell and Alphonso Taft (father of President William Howard Taft). The origin mythology connects it to a German secret society (possibly a Masonic or Illuminati-connected order at the University of Mainz). 15 Yale seniors are tapped each year. They are known as "Bonesmen." Their meetings take place in "The Tomb" — a windowless brownstone building on High Street, New Haven owned by the Russell Trust Association, a Skull and Bones holding entity incorporated in Connecticut in 1856. The building is sealed from public access and from Yale University governance.

The seizure documents are part of the US National Archives. The Thyssen-Bush financial relationship — connecting Skull and Bones money to Nazi industrial financing — is confirmed by primary source documents, not speculation.

Order 322 — The Number

The "322" in Skull and Bones numerology is interpreted in two ways: it refers to Bones founder William Russell (class of 1833 — founded the second year after the referenced class of 1832); and it references Demosthenes, who died in 322 BC and whom the order honours as the embodiment of rhetoric as power. The skull-and-crossbones is not decorative — it is the symbol of the Knights Templar and appears on Masonic-tradition aprons in specific degree rituals. (See: Knights Templar)
