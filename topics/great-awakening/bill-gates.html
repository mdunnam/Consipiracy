The 2010 TED Talk — In His Own Words

"The world today has 6.8 billion people. That's headed up to about 9 billion. Now if we do a really great job on new vaccines, health care, reproductive health services, we could lower that by perhaps 10 or 15 percent." — Bill Gates, TED talk, February 2010

This statement is not disputed. It is on video. Gates is explicitly connecting vaccine deployment and healthcare to population reduction. The statement has been widely "fact-checked" as meaning that improved healthcare reduces birth rates — but this interpretation does not account for the explicit logical structure: "If we do a great job on vaccines... we could lower that [population trajectory]." The charitable interpretation is forced. The statement means what it appears to mean.

The Gates Foundation holds significant shares in Pfizer, BioNTech, Moderna, and CureVac — the companies whose vaccine products the WHO recommended for global deployment during COVID-19. This is a disclosed-but-unaddressed conflict of interest of extraordinary scale.
