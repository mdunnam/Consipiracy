The Van Allen Belt Problem

The Van Allen radiation belts are two toroid-shaped zones of charged particles (electrons and protons) trapped by Earth's magnetic field. The inner belt begins at approximately 1,000 km altitude and extends to roughly 6,000 km. The outer belt extends from about 13,000 km to 58,000 km. Between them is the South Atlantic Anomaly, where the inner belt dips closest to Earth's surface.

These belts were discovered in 1958 by Explorer 1 — the first successful American satellite — which measured radiation so intense that its Geiger counter went off the scale. Subsequent measurements confirmed radiation doses within the inner belt that would deliver a lethal dose to an unshielded human in approximately 30 minutes.

The Apollo missions claim to have passed through the Van Allen belts on each lunar transit — a journey through the belts taking approximately 30 minutes each way. The Apollo spacecraft's aluminium walls provided approximately 2 millimetres of shielding. Modern radiation scientists calculate that this shielding would have reduced belt exposure to approximately 25 rems per hour — still a significant dose for the claimed transit time. NASA's own post-mission health monitoring of Apollo astronauts has been cited by researchers as inconsistent with significant radiation exposure.

NASA Engineer Admits the Problem (2014)

In 2014, NASA engineer Kelly Smith narrated a promotional video for the Orion deep-space capsule, stating: "We must solve [the Van Allen belt radiation problem] before we send humans through this region of space." This official NASA admission — that the Van Allen belt radiation must be "solved" before humans can safely transit it — directly contradicts the claim that twelve Apollo astronauts transited it safely in 1969-1972 in craft with far less shielding than Orion.

The Moon Landing: The Evidence

The Apollo moon landings are the most scrutinised claimed achievements in human history. Researchers from every discipline — photography, physics, rocket engineering, retroreflector optics, geology, and film production — have published documented anomalies that, taken collectively, seriously challenge the official account.

Flag Movement in Vacuum

In multiple Apollo footage clips, the American flag continues to ripple and wave for several seconds after being touched. In a vacuum — as the lunar surface allegedly is — there is no air to carry a ripple. The flag should stop moving the instant the astronaut's hand leaves it.

Identical Horizon Backgrounds

Researchers have identified multiple Apollo photographs showing different foreground subjects but identical background landscapes — suggesting a backdrop was used rather than an actual location photographed from different angles.

Perfect Lighting

On the Moon, with no atmosphere to diffuse light, shadows should fall in perfectly parallel lines from the single light source (the sun). Multiple Apollo photos show shadows converging or diverging — consistent with nearby studio lighting rather than a single distant sun.

Missing Radiation Fogging

Photographic film is extremely sensitive to radiation. The radiation environment between Earth and the Moon — even excluding the Van Allen belts — should have produced significant fogging on unpressurised film canisters stored in the lunar module. Apollo images show no such fogging.

The Dutch Moon Rock

A 89-gram "Moon rock" gifted by NASA to the Netherlands after Apollo 11 was examined by the Amsterdam Rijksmuseum in 2009 and confirmed by geologists to be petrified wood of terrestrial origin. NASA had no explanation.

Lost Telemetry Data

NASA has admitted that the original telemetry tapes from Apollo 11 — the primary data record of the moon landing — were lost in the 1970s. The agency destroyed the original Slow-Scan Television conversion equipment used to broadcast the landing footage. The primary evidence of the most important achievement in human history was erased and cannot be recovered.

The ISS Deception

The International Space Station is presented as humanity's permanent orbital outpost, hosting continuous human habitation since November 2000. Researchers examining ISS footage systematically identify anomalies consistent with film production rather than genuine microgravity:

  • Hair behaviour: Female astronauts' hair frequently behaves inconsistently with genuine microgravity — falling downward in some frames, while styled upward in others. True microgravity causes hair to float uniformly outward in all directions. The transition between these states mid-clip suggests a harness-based studio setup.
  • Air bubbles in spacewalks: Multiple reviewed EVA (Extra-Vehicular Activity) clips show spherical air bubbles emerging from helmets and suits and rising upward — consistent with underwater neutral buoyancy tank filming, not vacuum.
  • Green-screen artefacts: Frame-by-frame analysis of some ISS footage reveals colour fringing and edge artefacts consistent with chroma key (green screen) compositing at the boundaries between astronaut suits and backgrounds.
  • The no-clip problem: Astronauts regularly work with small objects and tools in genuine microgravity without securing them, yet these objects never float away during complex operations — suggesting they are on a surface rather than in free space.

No Real Photos of Earth

Despite having operated thousands of satellites and space missions since 1957, NASA has produced exactly zero unmanipulated photographs of the complete spherical Earth. Every image presented as a "photograph" of the full Earth is either a computer-generated composite (assembled from thousands of separate satellite image passes) or a digital artwork.

NASA's own image metadata confirms this. The famous "Blue Marble" image from Apollo 17 — one of the most reproduced photographs in history — was confirmed by NASA's own imaging team to be a "recreation" assembled from multiple sources, not a single photograph. The modern "Blue Marble" images released by NASA's EPIC camera are explicitly labelled as "natural colour" composites: colour-corrected and assembled from multiple spectral bands.

Flat Earth researchers note that across the series of "Blue Marble" official NASA Earth composites from different years, the size of North America varies dramatically — inconsistent with images of a fixed-size object photographed from a consistent altitude, consistent with different artists creating different composites without a consistent scale reference.
